Random insights regarding learning

There is a limit to how much you can learn, but there is no limit to how fast you can learn.

You have to optimize between learning speed and learning capacity

  • Things learnt fast have low compression.
  • You need sleep to compress and make space for newer things, and you need to not sleep to keep ingesting new stuff.
  • Be sensitive to the feleing when the brain goes into pain, its an indicator that it's full and needs to be left alone to compress.
  • A hack to get around the total learned volume limit is to truly understand the thing. That compresses the information to the smallest volume possible. The only drawback is: it's like extracting a highly compressed archive: decompression takes more time.
  • Such tight compression means that everytime you have to get to a specific conclusion, you have to think up a longer derivative chain: from the compressed root till the decompressed leaf.
  • Over time, with practice, the brain can get fast enough to decompress fast, so that the tight packaging has minimal side effects to speed.
  • I have felt that this 'decompression' process is generalistic: it exists as raw processing power. You get good at decompressing everything related/ similar to the practiced thing, not just a single thing.
  • Leverage this thing to get good at related things fast.

Everything get better after sleep

  • Use sleep as a way of reinforcement. Sleep when you feel like it.
  • I have 'learnt' more things when I have slept than when I was awake.

Intelligence is the ability to compress and then unpack

  • Had this insight in 2018 out of nowhere. Wrote a shit ton of the things I realized then. Didn't actually know shit about ai then.
  • I'm deeply ocnvinced about intelligence being the ability to compress without loss of information. Such compression seeks understanding of the entire body. It has a form of recursion built into it.
  • I was already convinced, but now I am confident after discovering the Hutter Prize.

The brain ingests/learns faster when challenged, compresses better when it's interested

  • Just another observation. Don;t have any formal proof besides experience.

Education

Packaged education is an idiot's concept

  • It's like minimum wage. The entire system with time will converge to the minimal prescribed syllabus and subjects.
  • There is no space or consideration for outliers: Those who do too badly to continue and those who do too well to continue.
  • It effectively kills the exploratory curiosity via its rigid defined path.
  • It's a consequence of commoditizing education. Commodities demand strict boundaries to enable packaging and determine completion of a process(in this case: of education, which is idiotic)
  • The only education I've gotten was the one that I gave myself (sadly).
