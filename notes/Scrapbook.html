Notes

  • Everyone has a glass ceiling.
  • The mental ceiling of a person is determined by the limits of his imagination. Limits of circumstances are secondary, and mostly don't matter.
  • Not everyone can look at things and the systems under them. Accept it and stop trying to teach poeple. Those who can, already know and understand.
  • Understand that technology works around 1-2 truly genius people systemizing things into existence, followed by hundreds of people subscribing to those ideas and further working on them.
  • These 1-2 people are the de-facto dictators of that branch of progess. Unless they die, in which case someone from the following body of the sphere starts rapidly understanding things and naturally ends up at the flesh-cutitng tip.
  • Innovation and invention is not a matter of if, but a matter of when. Max to max a person can do is make that thing exist faster into the present, than someone bringing it into existence in the distant future.
  • Innovation generally occurs in parallel, isolated, independent streams irrespective of their spatial location on the planet.

Career algorithm

  1. The first step is to find someone on the team and ask for 30 minutes with them.
  2. In that meeting you have a simple agenda: 1 For the first 25 minutes: ask them to tell you everything they think you should know. Take copious notes. Only stop them to ask about things you don’t understand. Always stop them to ask about things you don’t understand. 2 For the next 3 minutes: ask about the biggest challenges the team has right now. 3 In the final 2 minutes: ask who else you should talk to. Write down every name they give you.
  3. Repeat the above process for every name you’re given. Don’t stop until there are no new names.

Visa stuff

O1 visa(USA)

  • Sources:
    • Harshita Arora: A guide on getting an O-1 visa
  1. Evidentiary Criteria:
    1. Awards
    2. Membership in exclusive groups
    3. Media and news about you
    4. Patents or something else original and significant
    5. Publications authored by you
    6. High remuneration (usually that means 2x the average salary of a person in your profession in your country, which is sourced from something like Glassdoor. So if you’re a software engineer from India where the average salary is $10k/year and if you had your app acquired for $20k+, you have satisfactory evidence for this criterion).
    7. Spoke at a conference/ panel or judged a hackathon
    8. Work experience somewhere in a non-intern role.
  2. O-1 requires that you either own ≤49% equity in the petitioning company or have an odd number of Board of Directions so that you can be fired.
  3. Costs
    1. $460 is the government filing fees
    2. $1225 is the premium processing fee (this is optional. If you want your O-1 to be processed in 15 calendar days instead of months, then you need to pay this fee)
    3. $500 for Stripe Atlas to incorporate your company, if you do
    4. $7,500 for a law firm to prepare your petition (this is also optional. And the lawyer fee can vary, but $7.5k is the standard, I’ve heard).
  4. Consequences
    1. You need to keep a paycheck stub that your employer gives you every month or biweekly or however often you’re paid. If you incorporated a company for O-1 purposes, this means that you need to make enough revenue to pay the promised salary from your company’s business bank account to your personal bank account.
  5. What's next after O-1?
    • EB-1! This visa has the same criteria as O-1 but is harder to get.
    • This visa gets you a green card and can be self-sponsored (unlike O-1 which needs to be employer sponsored)
    • After getting a green card, you can apply for citizenship.
    • And you need to wait at least 90 days after entry on O-1 before you can petition for EB-1. Plus, you can’t travel internationally for 90 days prior to filing for EB-1.
    • If you don’t want to do EB-1, then you can extend your O-1 after the 3-year validity. My lawyer told me that you can extend it yearly, indefinitely, as long as you can prove that you’re adding value to the petitioning company (GitHub commits to show progress on the project would work if there’s nothing public/tangible).
  6. What can I do to improve my chances of getting O-1?
    • Apply to speak at conferences, go to hackathons and ideally win some, tap into your network to find important people in your field to get a letter of recommendation from, cold-email journalists to write an article about you and your work, win awards, etc. And don’t forget to document it all in a Google Doc!
